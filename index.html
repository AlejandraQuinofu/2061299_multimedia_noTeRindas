¿who are we?

We are a small group of students from the SENA, of which we have created a campaign that seeks to raise awareness in the youth of the 11th grade of Kennedy School, between the ages of 17 and 18, raising awareness of the problems that cause dependency and excess of drugs, this is directed; either for young consumers or non-consumers and relatives or close people who are affected with this problem.

Objective

Faced with the problem presented by excessive drug use in adolescents at the Kennedy School in Medellín, which is generating low performance in their grades, family and social problems, internal conflicts of young people and the non-acceptance of them, our campaign project "Do not give up", seeks to create a culture in young people, family members and people around who are affected by the use of drugs and become aware that they do not need them to solve problems or be fashionable and make people aware that have similar problems and feel affected.

With this we seek to improve the academic performance of young people, achieve a better family and social environment, acceptance of themselves, inform and sensitize young people about the importance of not depending on drugs. Making them see how this can have great consequences for their quality of life.
